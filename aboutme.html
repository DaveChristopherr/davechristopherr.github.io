About me

My name is Dave Christopher, also known online as Dave Pham. I am an aspiring developer with a strong interest in philosophy. I am particularly inspired by the ideas of Albert Camus and his reflections on human perseverance in The Myth of Sisyphus.

I began using the name Dave Pham online because I was a fan of Hanni. Through that community, I met many friends on the internet. From 2024 to 2025, I spent most of my time at home and often felt isolated, with few opportunities to socialize in person. Despite this, the friends I met online helped me maintain a sense of connection during difficult times.

I also experience social anxiety, which sometimes makes it difficult for me to start conversations or feel comfortable in social situations. Because of this, forming close friendships in real life has often been challenging. In many ways, the internet became a place where I could communicate more freely and connect with others without the pressure I usually feel in face-to-face interactions.

In the past, I struggled with Intermittent Explosive Disorder (IED), which made it difficult for me to control my anger when my emotions became overwhelming. There were moments when this led to serious conflicts with people around me. I deeply regret those situations and the pain they may have caused.

Over time, I began working to better understand myself and improve how I manage my emotions. The process has not been easy, and I still sometimes struggle when communicating with others. However, around 2025 to 2026, I slowly started reconnecting with people.

Growing up, I was very religious and regularly attended church. I also enjoyed watching shows about Jesus, such as The Bible. As life became more difficult, I began questioning my beliefs and wondering whether God truly exists. Around 2022, these questions eventually led me to identify as an atheist, although I still carry many unanswered questions about faith and existence.

Despite everything I have experienced, I continue moving forward. Today, I spend much of my time studying philosophy, playing chess, and learning programming. While I may not yet have all the answers, I continue to follow my own path while searching for meaning in life.
