About me

I am Dave Christopher, also known online as Dave Pham. I am an aspiring developer with a strong interest in philosophy. I am especially inspired by the ideas of Albert Camus and his reflections on human perseverance in The Myth of Sisyphus.

I used the name Dave Pham online because I was a fan of Hanni, and through that community I met many friends on the internet. From 2024 to 2025, I spent most of my time at home. During that time, I often felt alone and did not have many opportunities to socialize with people in person. However, the friends I met online helped me feel a sense of connection even during difficult moments.

I am also a socially anxious person, which means that I often find it difficult to start conversations or feel comfortable in social situations. Because of this, building close friendships in real life has been challenging for me. In many ways, the internet became a place where I could communicate more freely and connect with others without the pressure I usually feel in face to face interactions.

I have also struggled with Intermittent Explosive Disorder (IED), which made it very difficult for me in the past to control my anger when my emotions became overwhelming. There were moments when my anger led to serious conflicts with people around me. I deeply regret those moments and the pain they may have caused.

Over time, I began trying to understand myself and improve how I manage my emotions. The process has not been easy, and even now I sometimes struggle when communicating with others. However, around 2025 to 2026, I slowly started reconnecting with people and began going outside more often.

There are still times when staying at home feels easier than interacting with society. At school, I sometimes experience bullying because I always wear a mask. To be honest, I am not entirely sure why I continue wearing it, but it may be connected to my lack of confidence. There are moments when I feel that I do not fully trust other people, and sometimes I struggle to trust myself as well.

My relationship with my family has also been complicated at times. Because of my past struggles with anger, some relatives began to see me as unstable. There were serious conflicts in the past that I deeply regret, and as a result, some family members distanced themselves from me.

Growing up, I was very religious. I attended church every Sunday and enjoyed watching shows about Jesus such as Superbook. As life became more difficult, I began questioning my beliefs and wondering whether God truly exists. Around 2022, these questions eventually led me to identify as an atheist, although I still carry many unanswered questions about faith and existence.

Despite everything I have experienced, I continue to move forward. I am still thinking, still searching for meaning, and still trying to improve myself. Today, I spend much of my time studying philosophy, playing chess, and learning programming. While I may not yet have all the answers, I continue to follow my own path while searching for meaning in life.

Why I Became An Atheist

My transition to atheism was rooted in a profound realization: despite my deepest efforts and prayers, the universe remained silent. It felt as though no matter what I did or how hard I tried, there was no divine intervention shaping the outcomes of my life.

More importantly, I yearned for genuine personal freedom. I wanted to live authentically and make choices without being weighed down by the fear of a deity I no longer believed to be real. Stepping away from religion allowed me to break free from those invisible constraints, conquer that instilled fear, and take full ownership of my own existence and morality.
