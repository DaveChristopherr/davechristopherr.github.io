Black Hole

The Ultimate Cosmic Void
The Inspiration

My intense fascination with Black Holes actually started from my love for biographies. Watching the brilliant film The Theory of Everything introduced me properly to the life and mind of Stephen Hawking, who is definitely up there as one of my absolute favorite scientists.

"Because there is a law such as gravity, the universe can and will create itself from nothing." — Stephen Hawking

Hawking was an unapologetic atheist who relied purely on logic and physics to explain existence. I deeply respect that, even if it earned him a lot of bashers who couldn't handle his straightforward views on reality. And well... let's not even get started on the ruthless internet memes and his name randomly popping up in wild controversies like the Epstein files, haha! But setting the internet chaos aside, his contributions to understanding the universe are unmatched.

The Beauty of the Void

As for the space object itself, a Black Hole is the perfect representation of the great unknown. It is a point in space where gravity is so absolute that the laws of physics break down, time practically stops, and not even light can escape.

For someone who constantly thinks about existence, meaning, and nothingness, the black hole is the literal embodiment of the void. It is terrifying, chaotic, yet perfectly logical—a true masterpiece of the universe.
