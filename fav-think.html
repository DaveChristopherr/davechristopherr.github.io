Life & Existence

The Endless Search for 'Why'
The Question That Haunts Me

Of all the things my mind wanders to, the most frequent is the fundamental question of our existence. I constantly find myself thinking about our meaning, or the lack thereof.

"Why are we even alive if we are only destined to die anyway?"

This is the paradox of human life. We are given consciousness, the ability to feel, to dream, and to question, yet it all comes with a definitive expiration date. It is a heavy realization—that no matter what we build or achieve, everything eventually fades into nothingness.

Facing The Reality

As someone who looks at the world without the comforting answers of religion, I don't believe there is a grand, pre-written purpose waiting to be discovered. The universe is indifferent to our longing for meaning.

But perhaps, that is exactly where the beauty lies. Knowing that our time is fleeting makes the act of living an act of rebellion. We exist, we observe, and we experience this brief flash of consciousness simply because we are here—even if the end is silence.
