Alexander the Great

The Conqueror of the Known World
The Man Behind the Legend

My fascination with Alexander the Great really deepened after watching a biographical documentary about his life. Seeing his strategic mind unfold on screen was incredible. He wasn't just a king who inherited a throne; he was a brilliant tactician who approached warfare much like a master playing a massive game of chess.

"I am not afraid of an army of lions led by a sheep; I am afraid of an army of sheep led by a lion."

His unyielding ambition and ability to conquer the known world by his early twenties is nothing short of extraordinary. He operated with a level of logic and drive that permanently altered the course of human history.

The Spread of Philosophy

What truly cements him as my favorite historical figure is his connection to the mind. He was personally tutored by one of the greatest thinkers in history, Aristotle.

Because of his education, Alexander didn't just bring destruction to the lands he conquered; he brought ideas. His massive empire became the vessel that spread Greek philosophy, logic, and culture across different civilizations. It is fascinating to think that a military genius was also responsible for laying the groundwork for the spread of philosophical thought.
