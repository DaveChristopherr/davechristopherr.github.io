Sisyphus

The Absurd Hero
The Philosopher's Hero

My fascination with Sisyphus does not stem purely from ancient Greek mythology, but rather from how the philosopher Albert Camus reinterpreted his story in his essay, The Myth of Sisyphus.

Condemned by the gods to push a massive boulder up a mountain for eternity, only to watch it roll back down just before reaching the top, Sisyphus represents the ultimate futile struggle. He is the symbol of human existence in an indifferent universe.

"The struggle itself toward the heights is enough to fill a man's heart. One must imagine Sisyphus happy."
Embracing the Absurd

What makes him my favorite figure is his defiance. Camus teaches us that the tragedy of Sisyphus is only a tragedy if he lets the hopelessness crush him. By accepting the absurdity of his fate and continuing to push the boulder anyway, Sisyphus creates his own meaning.

To me, he is a reminder that even when the world lacks inherent purpose or comforting answers, we can still find strength, rebellion, and quiet victory in the act of living itself.
